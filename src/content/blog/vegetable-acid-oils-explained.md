Vegetable acid oils sit in the same family as PFAD but come from different crops — soya, cottonseed and rice bran — and buyers who only know palm-based feedstock often overlook them.

## What they are

Like PFAD, vegetable acid oils are produced during the refining of crude vegetable oil, when free fatty acids are stripped out to leave a food-grade oil behind. Soya acid oil, cottonseed acid oil and rice bran acid oil are the three most commonly traded — each carrying the fatty-acid profile of its source crop, which affects how it behaves in esterification and downstream processing.

## Why buyers diversify into them

For a refiner sourcing high-FFA feedstock for biodiesel, relying on a single crop or region concentrates risk. Vegetable acid oils sourced from different crops and geographies give a feedstock book more resilience against a single supply shock, while offering broadly comparable processing economics to PFAD.

## Quality considerations

FFA content, moisture and unsaponifiable matter all vary by crop and by refinery source, so a certificate of analysis per batch is essential — the numbers on offer sheets can drift from what actually loads. Buyers should also ask which crop and country of origin is behind a given parcel, since availability and pricing move independently across soya, cottonseed and rice bran.
