Every feedstock offer sheet quotes a price against a shipping term, and for buyers newer to the waste-oil trade, the difference between FOB and CIF is worth understanding before it affects a landed-cost comparison.

## FOB: the buyer takes on freight

Free On Board means the seller's responsibility ends once the cargo is loaded at the origin port — in SafOil's case, typically a Malaysian port. From that point, the buyer arranges and pays for ocean freight, insurance and onward logistics to the discharge port. FOB suits buyers who already have freight relationships and want direct control over vessel scheduling and insurance terms.

## CIF: the seller arranges freight to your port

Cost, Insurance and Freight bundles ocean freight and marine insurance into the seller's quote, with the cargo delivered to the buyer's named discharge port. CIF suits buyers without an established freight desk, or those who simply prefer one counterparty responsible for the cargo until it reaches their port — at the cost of less direct control over carrier choice.

## Comparing offers correctly

The most common mistake is comparing an FOB price from one supplier against a CIF price from another as if they were equivalent. They aren't — freight and insurance can move the true landed cost meaningfully, especially on tank or flexitank cargoes with heating or handling requirements like PFAD or tallow. Always normalise offers to the same term before comparing.
