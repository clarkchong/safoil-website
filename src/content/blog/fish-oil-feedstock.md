Fish oil is the least talked-about feedstock in the waste-lipid trade, and one of the most interesting for buyers looking beyond the usual UCO and palm-residue streams.

## A residue of the fishing and processing industry

Most traded fish oil is recovered as a by-product of fish processing and fishmeal production — a genuine residue stream rather than a purpose-grown crop, which is exactly what makes it attractive to buyers chasing waste-based classification. Its fatty-acid profile differs from vegetable and used-cooking-oil feedstocks, which matters for esterification chemistry and for blending decisions at the refinery.

## Where demand is coming from

Renewable diesel producers value fish oil for the diversity it brings to a feedstock slate, and as sustainable aviation fuel mandates phase in, refiners are widening their search for any credible waste-derived lipid that can support hydroprocessing routes. Fish oil sits squarely in that expanding pool.

## What to check before contracting

Odour, oxidation stability and FFA content vary significantly by source and by season, so per-batch laboratory testing is non-negotiable. Buyers should also confirm the specific fishery or processing origin, since traceability documentation for marine feedstocks is scrutinised closely at audit.
