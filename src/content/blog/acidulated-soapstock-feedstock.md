Acidulated soapstock rarely gets its own conversation, but for buyers already working with PFAD and vegetable acid oils, it's worth knowing where this heavier by-product fits.

## Where it comes from

During vegetable oil refining, the neutralization step removes free fatty acids as soapstock, which is then treated with acid to split it into a fatty-acid-rich oil — acidulated soapstock — and an aqueous phase. The result carries a higher FFA content than PFAD and a heavier, more viscous consistency, reflecting the extra processing step behind it.

## How it compares to PFAD and acid oils

The chemistry places it in the same broad category as PFAD and vegetable acid oils, suited to esterification routes and pre-treatment-capable HVO plants, but its higher FFA and heavier handling profile mean it is typically priced and contracted as a distinct grade rather than interchangeably with PFAD.

## What to check before contracting

FFA content, moisture and impurities should always be confirmed by a certificate of analysis rather than an offer-sheet estimate, since acidulation quality varies by refinery. Because the material is heavier than PFAD, buyers should also confirm heated tank or vessel logistics are built into the offer rather than assumed.
